**BUSINESS AND MANAGEMENT MODULE – REFLECTIVE ESSAY**

Participating in this business and management module has been a transformative learning experience for me. Before attending this module, my attitude towards entrepreneurship was mostly negative and filled with fear. I saw entrepreneurship as something extremely stressful, uncertain, and only meant for people with a lot of money or special connections. I believed it involved too much risk, slow income, and constant struggle, which made me feel discouraged and unwilling to explore it.

However, after completing this module, my mindset has significantly changed. I now understand that entrepreneurship is not just about starting a business, but about solving problems, creating value, and developing yourself. My fear has reduced, and I now feel more open, curious, and willing to learn and grow in this area. I have come to realize that with the right mindset, knowledge, and discipline, entrepreneurship can be a powerful path for personal and financial growth.

One of the most interesting parts of the module for me was learning about self-awareness and personal development. I learned that before building a business, it is important to understand yourself. The concept of the two dimensions of self-awareness—**intrapersonal** (understanding yourself) and **interpersonal** (understanding others)—helped me reflect deeply on my strengths, weaknesses, emotions, and how I relate with people. This made me realize that success in business is not only about ideas but also about emotional intelligence.

Closely related to this was the concept of emotional intelligence, which includes self-awareness, self-regulation, relationship management, and self-consciousness/self-knowledge. These elements helped me understand how to manage my emotions, communicate better, and build stronger relationships with others. I found this very important because in real life, especially in business, dealing with people is unavoidable.

Another concept that had a strong impact on me was the “zones” framework: comfort zone, fear zone, learning zone, growth zone, and champion zone. This helped me reflect on where I currently am and where I need to be. I realized that I have been mostly operating in my comfort zone, avoiding risks because of fear. However, the module taught me that real growth happens when you move into the learning and growth zones. This has encouraged me to start taking small steps outside my comfort zone.

The facilitator also introduced the “4Es” that shape an individual: environment, education, experience, and exposure. This made me reflect on how my background and surroundings have influenced my thinking and decisions. It also made me realize that I have the power to improve my environment, seek better education, gain experience, and expose myself to new opportunities in order to grow.

Another key learning point was the different types of capital and resources needed in entrepreneurship. Before now, I believed capital only meant money. However, I learned about human capital (skills and knowledge), social capital (relationships and networks), and experiential capital (experience gained over time). This changed my perspective completely because I now understand that even without a lot of money, I can still build something valuable by using my skills, connections, and experiences.

The framework of thought, particularly the HCT model (History, Context, and Trends), also stood out to me. It showed me how our mindset is shaped by our past experiences, current situation, and what is happening around us. I also learned about layers of context such as GLOCAL (global and local thinking), PESTEL (political, economic, social, technological, environmental, and legal factors), and VUCA (volatility, uncertainty, complexity, and ambiguity). These frameworks helped me understand how external factors affect business decisions and why it is important to think broadly and strategically.

Another important area I found interesting was entrepreneurial logic. I learned about predictive logic (planning based on expected outcomes) and control logic (taking action with available resources). The principle of effectuation taught me that entrepreneurs do not always wait for perfect conditions but start with what they have and adapt along the way. This made entrepreneurship feel more realistic and achievable for me.

The module also covered business models and value proposition. I learned that a good business must clearly define its offering, target audience, value, and differentiation. I also understood the importance of channels, customer relationships, and revenue streams. These concepts helped me see how businesses are structured and how they make money.

My experience during the in-class exercises and group work was both challenging and rewarding. Working with others helped me develop my communication and teamwork skills. At first, it was difficult to align ideas, but as we continued, I learned how to listen, contribute, and collaborate effectively. This experience showed me the importance of teamwork in building a successful business.

The delivery of the module was very engaging and practical. The facilitator explained concepts clearly and used real-life examples, which made it easier to understand. The interactive nature of the sessions, including discussions and activities, made the learning process enjoyable and effective. The facilitator also encouraged participation, which helped me build confidence in expressing my ideas.

Moving forward, I intend to apply the knowledge gained in several ways. First, I will work on improving my self-awareness and emotional intelligence, as these are important for both personal and business success. I will also start identifying problems around me and think about possible business solutions.

Additionally, I plan to start small by developing a simple business idea using the concepts of value proposition and business model. I will also focus on building my human and social capital by learning new skills and connecting with people who can support my growth.

In conclusion, this business and management module has had a significant impact on my mindset, knowledge, and personal development. It has changed my perception of entrepreneurship from something fearful to something achievable and meaningful. I am grateful for the knowledge gained and I am motivated to apply it in my life moving forward.
